26th August, 2016.

NETPIL - FIAN Collaboration

NETPIL had a collaboration with FIAN International, an international NGO that has done work on the Kaweri Coffee Company - land evictions case in Mubende. NETPIL provided a platform for FIAN to screen a documentary on the case and interface with the Media in a Press conference on developments in the case. The two activities run on Friday 26th August 2016.

2nd September, 2016

Annual General Meeting


The AGM to close the project year that ended August 2016 was held on 2nd September 2016. We had a registered attendance of 41members (19m, 22f). The members were briefed on the progress of the network and the future.

NETPIL together with the Equal Opportunities Commission convened Parallel Session V during the 3rd Annual National ESCR which ran between 14th - 15th September. The panel discussion considered "The feasibility of use of quasi-judicial mechanisms in Public Interest Litigation in the wake of human rights abuses by Transnational Corporations". The panelists included Peter Magera - Chapter Four, Arnold Kwesiga - Uganda Consortium on Corporate Accountability (UCCA); James Zeere - Centre for Health, Human Rights and Development (CEHURD); and Bernaddette Nalule- Equal Opportunities Commission. We still await the publication of the Conference proceedings.

29th September, 2016

Media Training on Land Law & Land Rights Reporting


The Advocacy Team under the Land Evictions & Natural Resources working group has conducted a 'Media Training on Land Law & Land Rights Reporting' on Thursday 29th September 2016 (81participants - 68m, 13f). The object of the training was to create a platform to engage the media and equip them with the prerequisite knowledge on land law and land rights, as well as equip them with skills on reporting on land rights issues. The forum was used to interest the media personnel in the Land Evictions PIL case.

7th October, 2016

Court Appearance for the case Muhindo James & 4ORS v AG Misc. Cause 127 of 2016.

NETPIL made the 1st court appearance on 7th October 2016 at the High Court Civil Division before lady Justice Patricia Basaza Wasswa. The case did not take off as the Attorney General had only filed his response to the application the previous day. No copy of the response was found on the court file. Matter was adjourned to 15th December 2016.
